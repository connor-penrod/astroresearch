Each field (database) corresponds to a given deep field in the sky included in the Rainbow database (e.g., GOODS, AEGIS, COSMOS, etc...). Select your field of interest from the drop menu, and then the "Selection Band" menu on the right will display the available catalogs.
Each band represents a source catalog based on a selection in a photometric band (e.g., R, K, [3.6], etc..) or survey (GOODS, SHARDS, CANDELS). All the information in the Rainbow database (photometry, redshifts, stellar population properties, etc..) refers to that sample of galaxies.

After you select an option from the drop menu, the footprint of the selection band in the chosen field along with the available surveys in that area will be shown in the frame on the right.

Basic information about the catalog is also given: central coordinates, version (date), number of sources in the catalog, and area covered.

After choosing a 'field' and 'selection band' in the menus above, click the button below.

A new page will open where you can query the Rainbow database and build a subsample of sources based on properties such as photometry, redshifts, colors, masses, SFRs, etc.).

You can also query the database for your own sample, uploading your own catalog which will be cross-matched (by coordinates) to the sources in the Rainbow database.

1) After selecting a field + band in the menus above, type the galaxy's R.A. and Declination (either in hours or degrees) and chose a search radius (in arcsec) to perform your search. If a match is found, you will be taken to the galaxy's page.

2) This page includes a summary of relevant photometric and stellar population parameters along with a Figure of the galaxy SED, and several postage stamps of the galaxy in different bands.

1) After selecting a field + band in the menus above, type the galaxy ID in the box to go straight to the galaxy's page.

2) This page includes a summary of relevant photometric and stellar population parameters along with a Figure of the galaxy SED, and several postage stamps of the galaxy in different bands.

WARNING: The galaxy ID should match the name of source in the Selection Band catalog that you have chosen above

NOTE: The default size of the postage stamps, the color of the background and some other options can be customized on the Postage stamp Menu at the bottom of this page.

Create a map of given size (in arcmin) using the Selection band image as reference.

Each source in the catalog will be displayed as a green dot. If you click on it you will be redirected to the galaxy's webpage containing all the relevant information about that source.

A list with all the sources identified within the region of the map is also given in the page.

NOTE: Once you select a Field+Band in the 2 top menus the RA and DEC boxes will be filled with the central coordinates of the catalog.

Some options might be selected to tweak what the Rainbow database will show.

By default, postage stamps are plotted with white background and sources in black. This can be changed with the "Invert B&W colors" checkbox.

Postage stamps can be shown with RA-DEC labels.

If you are only interested in taking a look at the SED, select "no postage stamps" (it will run faster).

Postage stamps for bands with no measured photometry can also be shown.
